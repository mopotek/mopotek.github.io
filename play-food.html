Technology for a social and emotional eating experience

2020 @Social and Emotional Technology Lab, UC Santa Cruz

User research • Participatory co-design • Speculative design

The problem

Mealtime has always been a great opportunity to strengthen bonds between people through the shared experience of eating that has sensorial and emotional dimensions.

Nowadays, people live very busy lives which can prevent them from being really present. More and more, technology is becoming a disruption when eating in a social context.

The objectives

  • Discover what playful things people already do during mealtime
  • Understand what kind of tech integrations during mealtime are desirable or disruptive to the dining experience
  • Create technology concepts that are playful and social

Which methods did we use?

Our international team of 3 (India and California) led by Ferran Altarriba Bertran employed the following methods. Due to the pandemic, we had to pivot from in-person activities and carried them out remotely!

Our research papers have not been published yet so I cannot disclose everything, but I will provide further details in the future!

1. Thematic analysis of an online survey

I did a thematic analysis of the online survey which allowed me to identify themes such as which types of uses of phones and technology create isolation from the social experience or a sensory distraction that detracts from mindfulness.

I also formulated design guidelines to help fellow designers and engineers to create technology that has a positive impact!

2. Cultural probes

With our preliminary results in hand, we recruited participants to do a booklet of 7 activities related to eating to do during the week, such as taking pictures of their kitchen, or telling us about their favorite eating experiences.

This allowed us to have an intimate look at people's food customs all around the world that gave us concrete building blocks for our designs.

3. Brainstormings and design sketching for ideation

After discovering many specific instances of playfulness and social connection from people's experiences around food, we used them to inspire our new technology concepts. Through several iterative brainstorming sessions where we sketched the contents of our brain on and on, we selected and refined a dozen of design concepts.

4. Remote play tests of our created concepts with users

Ferran put those concepts in a speculative design catalog that illustrated and explained all of our ideas (see images below).

We then distributed this catalog to people to find out which ideas they thought were best and tested them out remotely.
