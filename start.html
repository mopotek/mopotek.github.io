Exercising playfully and creatively, free of worries

User research • UX / UI design • Usability evaluation

2017 @Graduate School of Cognitive Engineering, Bordeaux

Many people are discouraged to start or maintain a physical activity. It's unfortunate as these people are missing out on the amazing benefits of exercising on mental and physical health!

Having personaly experienced mental blocks regarding my physical performances that prevented me to actually exercise, I wanted to explore a new approach completely free of worries and judgment.

I had the intuition that we had to provide another reason to workout with a tool that brought intrinsic pleasure.

The objectives

  • Understand what really drives and discourages people to exercise
  • Create a tool that will help people make physical activity a natural and pleasurable priority in their life

Which methods did we use?

I was the project manager of a team of six. We employed the following methods.

1. Open-ended interviews and personas

We interviewed 10 people from different backgrounds to ask them about their attitudes and habits towards exercising to better understand their motivations and roadblocks.

After analyzing this data, we were able to create personas that represent the different types of attitudes and habits towards exercising that we observed. This allowed us work on our empathy to keep all types of users in mind when ideating and prototyping.

2. Contextual research and ideation

The main points that stoud out were the necessity for a social component in the practice and the variety of motivations: while some adore competing against people, others will find it completely offputting and are looking for more play.

We did an inventory of all apps that allowed for a gamified exercising experience, while also looking for ways people were using alternative motivations to work out.

The practice of GPS Drawing, or running in order to create a GPS trace representing something, stood out.

3. Ideation and prototyping of an app

We decided to create a system that made the practice of GPS Drawing more immersive. At this point, runners had their nose planted in their map, which endangered their safety, and also had to draw their whole itinerary by hand. We went on a bodystorming run to experience the constraints of outdoor running first hand and ideate on the spot.

After coming up with the app idea, I created a customer journey map to better understand in which context the interaction with the system would happen. Finally, I did a first iteration of a dynamic prototype with Axure RP that reprensented the app concept.

4. Usability user testing

We went through rounds of user tests where users would try out different scenarios within the app while I observed different qualitative and quantitative metrics such as number of errors, body language and verbatims... The experience was followed by a UEQ usability questionnaire as well as open-ended questions.

Analyzing this feedback allowed me to identify the main flaws of the system and to create a more usable and accessible version.
