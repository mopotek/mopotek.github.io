An entryway for Everyday Speech's products

UX design • Project management

2022, for Everyday Speech

This company's potential customers' knowledge on their products was very low, meaning that they heavily relied on live interactions with CX teambers to purchase the right product for them.

This led CX teams to not put in the effort on those clients who would bring in the most revenue.

For this re-design of a portion of the marketing website, I created a flexible experience where the user can either quickly self-direct to a specific product for purchasing and self-education, or be guided to the best product for them via an identification quiz.

The objectives

  • Better understand who the user is and what stage of the buying process they are in to personalize their experience
  • Provide more information more flexibly so the user has fewer decisions to make in order to see what we offer. #1 goal: help 100% of users understand which product is right for them
  • Allow high-intent users to accomplish the tasks they want - starting a free trial, generating a quote, booking a call
  • Provide an outlet for lower-intent users to self-educate more robustly before asking them to meet

On the right, the intial version of the experience.

UX Design process

1. Building understanding

After understanding the CEO's and Director of CX's understanding of the problems on our website, I established that my suggested solution needed to respect the following criteria:

  1. The potential customer must be able to know immediately that 3 different products are available and get a rough idea of their pricing. This was, if they are already self-informed, it will be easy for them to choose the one they want and initiate a free trial.
  2. If the user is not well informed, we guide them to the best product, depending on their use scenario. For example, a speech-language pathologist teaching social emotional learning in a 1:1 setting will be directed to the Social Communications curriculum.
  3. The information displayed on the results page must be different depending on who is browsing it: administrative personnel must see more information about purchasing logistics rather than sample materials.

Disclaimer: for this project, I was not able to do primary user research, meaning that I received all the context from our internal stakeholders and was lacking user's perspective on which type of experience they were looking for or the issues with the current one.

2. Ideation

After investigating how several websites presented their offers and consulting the psychology of price and offer display, I started low-fi ideation and came up with 3 versions of the experience:

  • Idea 1: ability to view all offers in one page + dynamically update the highlighted offer depending on the user’s parameters
  • Idea 2: ability to select one offer to the get to the conversion action
  • Idea 3: a questionnaire that will guide the customer to the right curriculum and present all its details while still allowing to see a preview of all plans

Option 3 was selected by the main stakeholders as it presented the core information for 3 products in a more synthetic way which still allowed more detailed and personalized information about 1 product once the user answered the quiz.

3. Iterate, iterate, iterate

I then proceeded with 2 design med-fi iterations of the concept. Feedback from stakeholders on the first version revealed the following main issues/needs, that were fixed in the next iteration:

  1. Quicker access to information if user does not want us to tell them about themselves
  2. Remove some complexity of the flow by fusing some experiences (e.g. show specialists that choose “multiple users” a lot of the same pricing options that we show admins)
  3. Improve the labeling of CTAs
  4. Improve premium offer highlighting
  5. Improve sample content visualization
  6. Show more product samples

4. Managing a UI freelancer

Once I was done with the UX work, a freelance UI designer took over the work for the high-fidelity designs. I was there along the way to guide his tasks, provide him with our design guidelines and feedback, and ensure everything was going according to the plan.
