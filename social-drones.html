Social drones to connect communities and play

Literature and market review • User research • Speculative design

2020 @Social and Emotional Technology Lab, UC Santa Cruz

We witness many utilitarian and functional applications of drones like in the military or agriculture, but the public opinion regarding them is very polarized due to legitimate ethical and security concerns.

Are we not missing out on ways these technological wonders could be used to promote social connection and community building, instead of always pushing a productivity agenda?

The objectives

  • Engage in speculative design to think about our desired or undesired future technology
  • Discover how people are already using drones in alternative playful or social ways and document this inexisiting knowledge
  • Imagine design concepts that benefit people's socio-emotional wellbeing

Which methods did I use?

I executed the following methods while my colleagues Ella Dagan Peled, Ferran Altarriba Bertran and Katherine Isbister held a reviewing and supervisation role.

1. Literature and market review

I reviewed 130 academic and online resources regarding the design space of social drones. This allowed me to detect which design trajectories remained unexplored and to which we could contribute to. I also reviewed different services and types of drones were present in the current drone market.

I concluded that most work concerned interaction modalities with drones and specific social drones concepts. However, no intermidiary knowledge existed regarding playful applications of drones as well as uses of drones to make people more social with each other!

So I set out to understand what principles of playful and social interaction with or around drones could be identified.

2. Bodystorming

Bodystorming is like brainstorming, but you add the environmental and physical elements of the design space you are exploring.

Basically, myself and other users experimented using various types of drones and observed which spontaneous and playful uses of drones came up! This gave me many actionable elements called Drone Play Potentials that I incorportated into our speculative design concepts.

In this video, you can observe an in-house fish-drone race that adapts to its environment with an added element of difficulty due to the fishes manoeuvering affordances (the fish is controlled by the movement of its tail and a shifting weight) and opened windows.

3. Social media review of drone uses

I went on Instagram, YouTube, and TikTok to discover more drone play potentials, or how people were already using drones in playful ways.

After a card sorting exercise that allowed us to categorize the play potentials from the bodystorming and social media exploration, we created several design directions for playful and social drone design as seen in the picture on the right.

We created a pictorial that presents our results, the design trajectories' underlying mechanisms.

4. Participatory ideation sketching

My colleagues and I went through several rounds of brainstorming and ideation sketching that allowed to come up with 10 speculative social drones concepts. They represent future uses of drones that could be developed, such as drones for icebreaking or to play social agility games.

5. Concept refinement

The final ideas were clarified and illustrated for a feature in the speculative design catalog you can find below. This design artifact itself represents the knowledge and ideas acquired during this project and in the future, could be used to gather opinions from potential users or industry players or to expand on the concepts on a collective manner, in a similar way as in the Play Food project.
