Social drones to connect communities and play

Literature and market review • User research • Speculative design

2020 @Social and Emotional Technology Lab, UC Santa Cruz

We witness many utilitarian and functional applications of drones like in the military or agriculture, but the public opinion regarding them is very polarized due to legitimate ethical and security concerns.

Are we not missing out on ways these technological wonders could be used to promote social connection and community building, instead of always pushing a productivity agenda?

The objectives

  • Engage in speculative design to think about our desired or undesired future technology
  • Discover how people are already using drones in alternative playful or social ways
  • Imagine design concepts that benefit people's socio-emotional wellbeing

Which methods did I use?

I executed the following methods while my colleagues Ella Dagan Peled, Ferran Altarriba Bertran and Katherine Isbister held a reviewing and supervisation role.

1. Literature and market review

I reviewed 130 academic and online resources regarding the design space of social drones. This allowed me to detect which design trajectories remained unexplored and to which we could contribute to. I also reviewed different services and types of drones were present in the current drone market.

I concluded more attention needed to be put towards playful applications of drones as well as uses of drones to make people more social with each other!

2. Bodystorming

Bodystorming is like brainstorming, but you add the environmental and physical elements of the design space you are exploring.

Basically, myself and other users experimented using drones and observed which spontaneous and playful uses of drones came up! This gave me a bunch of actionable elements that I incoportated into our speculative design concepts.

3. Social media review of drone uses

I went on Instagram, YouTube, and TikTok to discover and catagorize how people were already using drones in playful ways.

We created a pictorial that presents our results and suggests design trajectories (currently under review at different publications).

4. Participatory ideation sketching

My colleagues and I went through several rounds of brainstorming and ideation sketching that allowed to come up with 10 speculative social drones concepts. They represent future uses of drones that could be developed, such as drones for icebreaking or to play social agility games.
